# Chapter 5: Perception and Sensing

## Introduction

Perception is the foundation of intelligent robotic behavior, enabling humanoid robots to understand and interact with their environment. Effective perception systems integrate multiple sensory modalities to create a coherent understanding of the world.

## Sensory Modalities

### Vision Systems
- Stereo vision for depth perception
- RGB-D cameras for color and depth
- Omnidirectional cameras for wide field of view
- Event-based cameras for high-speed perception

### Auditory Perception
- Sound source localization
- Speech recognition and understanding
- Environmental sound classification
- Acoustic scene analysis

### Tactile Sensing
- Force and torque sensing
- Texture and material recognition
- Contact detection and localization
- Slip detection

### Proprioception
- Joint position and velocity sensing
- Inertial measurement units
- Torque sensing in actuators
- Whole-body state estimation

## Sensor Integration

### Sensor Fusion
- Kalman filtering approaches
- Particle filtering for non-linear systems
- Bayesian inference methods
- Deep learning fusion networks

### Multi-Modal Processing
- Cross-modal attention mechanisms
- Joint representations of sensory data
- Consistency checking across modalities
- Robustness to sensor failures
